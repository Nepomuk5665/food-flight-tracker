# Presentation Flow

## 1. Introduction
- Start by introducing the app and its main purpose.
- Explain that the platform helps users track food products from scanning to detailed supply chain insights.
- Mention that the app is designed for both consumers and administrators.

---

## 2. Finding a Product
- Begin on the main screen of the app.
- Show how a user can search for or discover a product.
- Explain that products can either be found manually or accessed directly by scanning.

**What to say:**
> The user starts by finding a product they want to learn more about. This is the entry point into the full tracking experience.

---

## 3. Scanning a Product
- Demonstrate the scanning feature.
- Show the user scanning a barcode or QR code on the product.
- Explain that the app instantly connects the scanned product to its stored data.

**What to say:**
> Once the product is scanned, the app identifies it and loads all available product information connected to that item.

---

## 4. Product Appears in History
- After scanning, move to the **Product History** section.
- Show that the scanned product is now saved in the history list.
- Explain that this makes it easy for users to return to previously scanned products.

**What to say:**
> Every scanned product is automatically stored in the product history, so the user can quickly access it again without scanning it a second time.

---

## 5. Opening Product Details
- Click on the product from the history section.
- Open the product details page.
- Show the information available there, such as:
    - Product name
    - Brand
    - Origin
    - Ingredients
    - Status
    - Tracking information

**What to say:**
> By selecting a product from the history, the user can view detailed information about that item, including where it comes from and other important product data.

---

## 6. Asking the AI a Question
- On the product details page, show the AI assistant feature.
- Ask a sample question about the product, such as:
    - “Is this product safe?”
    - “Where does this ingredient come from?”
    - “Are there any incidents related to this product?”

- Show the AI responding with useful information.

**What to say:**
> The built-in AI assistant allows the user to ask natural questions about the product and receive quick, understandable answers based on the available data.

---

## 7. Switching to the Admin Dashboard
- Transition from the consumer-facing product view to the **Admin Dashboard**.
- Explain that the dashboard gives a more advanced overview of product data.
- Show that admins can access deeper operational and tracking details.

**What to say:**
> While the product page is designed for normal users, the admin dashboard provides a more detailed and powerful view for monitoring the full supply chain.

---

## 8. Viewing More Product Information in Admin Dashboard
- Show how the same product can be viewed inside the admin dashboard.
- Present additional admin-level data, such as:
    - Internal product records
    - Supply chain timeline
    - Batch information
    - Incident history
    - Status monitoring

**What to say:**
> In the admin dashboard, the product can be analyzed in much more depth, making it possible to monitor logistics, quality, and safety information.

---

## 9. Working with Batches
- Demonstrate how the product is connected to **batches**.
- Show that one product may belong to one or multiple tracked batches.
- Explain that batch tracking helps identify where a product has been and what events are connected to it.

**What to say:**
> Batch tracking is important because it allows the system to follow not just the product itself, but the exact production group it belongs to.

---

## 10. Incident Reports
- Open the **Incident Report** section.
- Show how issues can be linked to specific products or batches.
- Explain examples such as:
    - Contamination warnings
    - Transport issues
    - Temperature problems
    - Recall alerts

**What to say:**
> If something goes wrong in the supply chain, incident reports make it possible to quickly identify affected products and batches.

---

## 11. Globe View / Global Map View
- End with the **Globe View**.
- Show the product, batch, or incident data displayed geographically.
- Demonstrate how locations across the supply chain are visualized on the map.
- Show how incidents and batch movement can be understood globally.

**What to say:**
> The globe view brings all the information together visually, showing how products move across locations and where incidents happen in the supply chain.

---

## 12. Closing Summary
- Recap the full journey:
    1. Find a product
    2. Scan the product
    3. Save it to history
    4. Open product details
    5. Ask AI questions
    6. Move to the admin dashboard
    7. Explore deeper product and batch data
    8. Review incidents
    9. Visualize everything on the globe view

**What to say:**
> This flow shows how the app works from a simple user scan all the way to advanced admin-level tracking, giving full transparency from product discovery to global supply chain monitoring.

---
